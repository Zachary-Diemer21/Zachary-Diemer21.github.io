About Me

Zachary Diemer

Hello! My name is Zachary Diemer and I am currently a senior in the Macaulay Honors College at the College of Staten Island. This brief introduction will most certainly take longer than a world class speed cuber solving a rubix cube, however, if you get bored I have provided a video of world renowned speed cuber, Lucas Etter below. He has consistently solved 3x3x3 cubes in under 6 seconds for the last three years, and recently, he even broke the 5 second mark.

I was born in Staten Island, New York. I initially planned to be a doctor in order to conduct research in the field of vascular diseases to hopefully find a cure for my mother's disease. However, at the end of my freshman year in college, a friend of mine on the quidditch team, introduced me to some extremely interesting topics and job statistics surrounding the cyber security realm. This initial discussion peaked my interest, so much so that later on that day I returned home and researched topics within this particular field for 12 hours. At this point, I decided to sit down and really think about the path that I was taking. After answering some tough questions, and discussing with some of my life mentors, I decided to make the decision to change my major to Mathematics/Computer Science. Such a change would allow me to combine a previous passion I had for finance with this newfound passion to create new and exciting software that could be utilized by small businesses to aid their business decisions. This then led me to take part in a particular internship with the Socioeconomic Policy Stimulation Center, which furthered my interest in the big data analytics space.

My current career aspirations are to become a data engineer at a particularly large company in NYC. I would like to take part in a team that designs, and implements real time analytical systems and/or applications for such a company, and hopefully benefit its large quantity of users. It may not sound as inspirational as finding a cure for avascular necrosis, however, it can still be an extremely fulfilling career that pushes my limits, brings forth new challenges, requires me to consistently learn, and most importantly allows me to be creative.
